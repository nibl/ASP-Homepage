Developers Rush in

As developers we want to build things. Presented with a problem, our minds immediately go into problem-solving mode. We figure out how to implement a solution with code. We rush in and build something. Then we discover people are not interested.

Will They Pay?

Our problem is we are so excited to build new features that we often forget our users' true needs. We end up spending time and resources developing features people don't really want or need, and ultimately won't pay for.

It started with what incredible benefits can we give to the customer, where can we take the customer. Not [...] what awesome technology we have and how we're going to market that.

-- Steve Jobs at WWDC, 1997.

Ask the Tough Questions

Feature Scrutinizer is an interactive application which asks the tough questions about each feature idea you have. It asks about:

  • User needs
  • Effects of changes to an existing user experience
  • Required development resources
  • Possible financial investments
  • Impact on your business

Receive Guidance

The amount of questions feature scrutinizer may ask depends on your answers. It will take the shortest route to providing quality guidance.

Feature Triage

After scrutinizing a proposed feature, you will know whether to:

  • Implement
  • Scrap
  • Postpone

Internalize the Process

It's important to learn and internalize this process. At every step to be considering how the feature will benefit users, and your business. Repeating this process creates an awareness of your software as part of a business. A software business that moves people forward.

Here's what Feature Scrutinizer can do for you:

  • Pick features worth your time and resources
  • Avoid feature creep
  • Focus on moving your users forward

Feature Scrutinizer saves development time, resources, and frustration by avoiding features that won't pay off.
